I've been sketching more lately — not with any purpose, just letting the pencil wander. It's a practice in surrender, in trusting the hand more than the mind. The best drawings happen when I stop thinking about what I want to make and start paying attention to what wants to emerge.

There's a sketchbook on my desk that I've been filling slowly over the past few months. Flipping through it is like reading a diary written in shapes rather than words. Some pages are dense with hatching and crosshatching, angry almost. Others are sparse — a single curved line, a circle, a suggestion of something that never fully materialized.

I think about the Japanese concept of *wabi-sabi* — the beauty of imperfection, of things incomplete and impermanent. My sketches are full of this quality. Lines that don't quite meet. Shapes that aren't quite symmetrical. Spaces left intentionally empty. There's a kind of honesty in imperfection that polish can never achieve.

Today I drew a leaf. Or rather, I drew something that started as a leaf and became something else entirely — more like a wave, or a breath made visible. I liked it better for not being what I intended.
